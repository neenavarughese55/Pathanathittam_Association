Places to visit in Pathanamthitta

Sabarimala

Approximately 80 km from Pathanamthitta town, Sabarimala, one of the most important religious centres in South India, is home to the Sastha Temple. This temple, dedicated to Lord Ayyappa, is located in the midst of the hazardous Western Ghats hills in a tranquil and natural setting.

Perunthenaruvi Waterwall

On the banks of the River Pamba lies a naturally occurring waterfall surrounding by beautiful scenery. The fall cascades down a rocky bed and is between 60 and 100 feet deep. Both residents and visitors adore this location for picnics.

Learn More

Muloor Smarakam

Muloor S Padmanabha Panicker (1869–1931), a poet and social reformer from the pre–independence era, is honoured at Muloor Smarakam. Situated in the little village of Elavumthitta, just 12 km away from Pathanamthitta town, this memorial is a typical Kerala house that was previously occupied by the poet.

Gavi

The Kerala Forest Development Corporation's eco-tourism project Gavi is situated in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala. The Pathanamthitta district's Gavi offers hiking, wildlife viewing, outdoor camping in specially constructed tents, and night safaris to its guests.

Aranmula

Aranmula is mostly recognised for its exquisite handiwork and the snake boat race that is held there on the last day of the Onam Festival. This is the area where the most famous metal mirror art of Aranmula Kannadi started.
