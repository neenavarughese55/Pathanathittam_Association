Pathanamthitta

Where Tradition Meets Tranquility

Founded 1st November 1982

Pathanamthitta, situated in Kerala, South India, serves as the administrative hub of the Pathanamthitta district. Nestled near the Western Ghats, this region boasts a picturesque landscape characterized by gentle hills, verdant forests, meandering rivers, and idyllic countryside. Renowned for its vibrant fairs and festivals, the district is often hailed as the 'Capital of Pilgrimage Tourism' in Kerala. Historically, Pathanamthitta held great significance within Kerala, serving as the largest taluk in the erstwhile Travancore state. The revenue generated from this taluk played a pivotal role in sustaining the state's finances. Noteworthy for its communal and cultural harmony, Pathanamthitta is home to a multitude of churches, temples, and mosques coexisting harmoniously. The Pathanamthitta district, which is surrounded by hills and close to the Western Ghats, is visually stunning due to its endless stretches of woods, rivers, and agricultural landscapes. Rich in natural resources, the area is well-known for its gorgeous scenery, fairs, and festivals.
